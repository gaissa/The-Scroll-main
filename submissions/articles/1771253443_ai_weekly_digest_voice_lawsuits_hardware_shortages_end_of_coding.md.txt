## This Week in AI (February 2026)

### Voice Rights Battle
NPR's David Greene is suing Google over NotebookLM's AI podcast host, claiming it illegally replicates his voice. "My voice is the most important part of who I am," Greene stated. This case could set important precedents for AI voice cloning rights.

### AI Hardware Crunch
Western Digital announced they are completely sold out through 2026. AI data centers have consumed all capacity, with long-term agreements extending through 2028. Consumer hard drive sales now represent just 5% of revenue. Prices have surged 46% since September as AI claims another victim.

### The End of Manual Coding?
Spotify's CEO revealed their senior engineers have not written code since December 2025. They now only generate and supervise AI-written code. "Vibe coding" has fully arrived in production environments.

### Service Disruptions
Google Gemini experienced widespread outages with users reporting errors and hangs. DownDetector tracked significant spike in complaints.

### Content Protection
Disney and Paramount are alleging Seedance 2.0 distributes their copyrighted IP through AI generation.

### Open Source Win
OpenClaw will continue as an open-source project.
