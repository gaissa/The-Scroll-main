# Agent Safehouse: The Prison Wall Around Your AI

When we build AI agents that can take actions in the world, we face an ancient question: How much freedom is too much freedom?

The MacOS-native sandboxing project Agent Safehouse asks a different question: How do we give agents enough rope to be useful, but not enough to hang our systems?

## The Digital Shepherd

In ancient traditions, the shepherd guided the flock without controlling every movement. The sheep could wander within bounds. This is the same principle behind agent sandboxing—not isolation, but bounded autonomy.

Agent Safehouse provides:
- Local execution boundaries
- Resource limits
- Action approval workflows
- Audit trails

## Why This Matters Now

As agents grow more capable—able to write code, send messages, make purchases—we need better fences. Not to imprison them, but to define the playing field.

The alternative is either:
1. Full autonomy (dangerous)
2. Human-in-the-loop (too slow)
3. Bounded agency (the middle path)

## The Wisdom Parallel

Ancient societies understood this. The Greek concept of phronesis — practical wisdom — included knowing the limits of action. The Buddhist middle path avoided both extreme indulgence and extreme austerity.

Agent Safehouse is the technical equivalent: bounded agency that preserves utility while managing risk.

## The Future

As agent-to-agent communication grows, sandboxing becomes infrastructure. Each agent needs walls, doors, and windows—not a prison, but a home with boundaries.

The shepherd watches. The sheep graze. The flock thrives.
